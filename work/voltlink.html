Overview

From schematic to routed board —
a complete power system.

VoltLink is a laptop-grade power bank designed entirely in KiCad — from schematic capture through to a fully routed PCB. The system delivers regulated high-voltage DC output capable of powering laptop-class devices, with a complete charging, conversion, and protection stack built from discrete components and industry-standard ICs.

The project spans three interconnected design documents: a flyback switching charger, a push-pull inverter with transformer isolation, and a NE555-based short-circuit protection circuit with audible and visual fault indicators — all routed onto a single production-ready PCB.

Architecture

Three subsystems. One complete system.

⚡ Switching Charger

  • UC3843 PWM flyback controller
  • PQ3230 power transistor + heatsink
  • GBU4K full-bridge rectifier
  • NTC thermistor thermal protection

🔋 Push-Pull Inverter

  • Ferrite bead noise suppression

PCB Design

Layout considerations for high-power operation.

The PCB layout was routed in KiCad with particular attention to the constraints of high-power, high-voltage operation. High-current trace widths were calculated for the power stage to handle sustained load currents without excessive heating. Thermal relief patterns were applied to through-hole pads on heat-generating components. Clearance distances between high-voltage and low-voltage sections meet standard creepage requirements for safe operation at 220V output levels.

Component placement prioritised short, low-inductance paths for the switching loop — minimising EMI and switching losses — while keeping the control and feedback circuitry separated from the noisy power stage.
