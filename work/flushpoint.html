The Edit

Storytelling in under
60 seconds.

FlushPoint is a short comedic sci-fi narrative edit — built to demonstrate the core skills of video post-production: narrative pacing, cut timing, audio-visual sync, and the ability to build tension and release it with a punchline reveal. The challenge was to tell a complete story — setup, conflict, and payoff — within a very short runtime.

The edit was produced entirely in DaVinci Resolve, covering the full post-production pipeline from rough assembly cut through to colour grade, audio mix, and final export. The tools used are fully transferable to OpenShot, Shotcut, and CapCut for platform-agnostic delivery.

Scene Breakdown

Three beats. One story.

1

The Incident — Girl in the bathroom

Our protagonist accidentally flushes something she shouldn't have — an alien that had been hiding in the toilet. The scene is played for comedic panic, with the flush as the point of no return.

2

The Investigation — Brother enters

Her brother walks into the bathroom looking for the alien. The tension builds as he searches — checking behind the door, scanning the room, opening the closet with growing suspicion.

3

The Reveal — Blue feathers in the drain

The punchline: a glimpse of vivid blue alien feathers slowly disappearing down the drain. The alien is gone. The brother is too late. Cut to black.

Good editing is invisible — you only notice it when it's bad. The goal with FlushPoint was to make every cut feel inevitable, every beat land at exactly the right moment, and let the blue feather do all the storytelling work at the end.
